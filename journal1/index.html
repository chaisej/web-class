In a world where the internet has become an integral part of our lives, the concept of a website takes on many meanings. J.R. Carpenter, Laurel Schwulst, and Ursula K. Le Guin each offer unique perspectives on the essence of websites, their purpose, and their potential future. Through this journal entry, I try to pull apart the ties of human creativity and the ever-evolving technology of websites.

"A Handmade Web" by J.R. Carpenter

A website as a handmade creation, akin to a work of art. The nostalgia of the early internet--where websites were crafted with care and intention, reflecting the personality and vision of their creators--was an exciting dawn. The web was a frontier of personal expression and exploration. Websites were not merely digital destinations but portals into the minds and hearts of their creators, a sentiment that I fear has been diluted in our current era of templates and homogenized designs.

"My Website is a Shifting House" by Laurel Schwulst

Websites as dynamic, ever-evolving spaces. Websites are homes that can be rearranged and transformed at will. Websites do not need to be static or clinical. They can and must evolve with us, adapting to our changing interests, experiences, and identities. A website is a living, breathing entity that can grow and change alongside us.
