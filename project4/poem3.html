Flurry On Passing Kolter
< back to the sandbox
Flurry
by me
reveal sandbox gems
hide sandbox gems
The light is bright
and fleeting
falling, descending
dying in the air like
an early December flurry
This is you
and me
and the ones we hold-this-close
and the ones we don’t
and all the mistakes
and the triumphs
This is you
like flurry
like supernova—
you a cyclone
a hothoused daydreamer
Yes, it will all fall away
and I am afraid, too
But now,
we have just begun
you with a wicked pen
you with many things to say
at first light
There is much to do
There is much for you to do
There is much for you to love
and to hold and to let go
When the final trumpet gives out
and our wings begin to melt
I will remember this
I will remember this kindness of yours

why I chose this poem
I wrote this poem for a friend of mine. It's the first that I've outright dedicated to someone. (Other poems are about people in my life, I just don't necessarily claim them in the footnotes!) I wanted to write a poem that was hopeful about life and that acknowledges how short it is. It was snowing earlier this month, and that image has stuck with me. I want to write more for people, and this is a good first step.
