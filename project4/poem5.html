Canary
by Rita Dove
reveal sandbox gems
hide sandbox gems
Billie Holiday’s burned voice
had as many shadows as lights,
a mournful candelabra against a sleek piano,
the gardenia her signature under that ruined face.
(Now you’re cooking, drummer to bass,
magic spoon, magic needle.
Take all day if you have to
with your mirror and your bracelet of song.)
Fact is, the invention of women under siege
has been to sharpen love in the service of myth.
If you can’t be free, be a mystery.

why I chose this poem
Rita Dove's diction is charming and unique. Through the plain text and through the audio rendition, Dove's humor and care for the subject at hand shines through. I also love hearing people explain their work before reading... It adds another layer for the audience. There is also a cunning edge to the work which I love!
