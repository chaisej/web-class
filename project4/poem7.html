Flurry On Passing Kolter
< back to the sandbox
On passing
by me
reveal sandbox gems
hide sandbox gems
It is easy to swallow the world
if you remember how much is already gone
how lovers have lost
how the seasons tire
Soon the Commander
who you’ve brushed against on the dancefloor
will call on you to rendezvous, to claim his soul tithe
But what a grandstand
you were here once—
wrote your name with hemlock bark on concrete
forgot a birthday
burnt the toast
misplaced the comma and your keys
stood unprotected in the storm
left the voicemail and another
ran for love
missed the bus
let a boy hold you by the shoulders
in a hotboxed bathroom
paid the fine on the way to Versailles—
What joy to fill the gap between nothings
with divine human error, protostar,
you were so many bright things
Constellations away, the pieces of you,
as if shepherded by divining rod, will reunite
but never same, never here with me
keeper of the black bird’s eye
The last dance is a death march, succumbed lullaby
he will lift you as if nothing—as if world of dust and unmarked bone
you will pull on his night-sky cloak, begging for aftertaste
At the end, you’ll crave the bellyache
you will ask for seconds

why I chose this poem
This is a new poem of mine. I think I often write about sad things or sad endings. This poem is definitely meant to be lighter. I feel like we hold the mistakes so close, as if they've become a part of us... We then punish ourselves for worrying about the mistakes. It's an endless give and take. I wanted to tackle missing even the embarassing parts of life in death. The important milestones are great, but what's more human to me is the mistakes. "Seconds" in the last stanza refers to seconds to time-craving more time even though life was hard-and second servings-even though the "bellyache" persisted it was worth it. Life is!!
