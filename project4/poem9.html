Flurry On Passing Kolter
< back to the sandbox
Kolter
by me
reveal sandbox gems
hide sandbox gems
I trust the radio
on the ride home
observe the dewy flatlands
where we feuded
over monkey bars
slipped notes
stood single file
there once a hallway
—hot and cold trysted,
ruined the village,
bore a silent plain,
no warning nor
tempest siren song
yet it pushed
everything over
but me—
it is a funny thing
that the thing
wishing to be gone
is standing
in the debris
is sitting in the car
looking at the remains
of a grade school,
let the prairie sing
it quivers and
a shadow
schoolhouse waves
let the prairie sing
it will sound
like the radio

why I chose this poem
This poem is about my elementary school which was ruined by Hurrican Harvey. Though I didn't visit the school often after "graduating," a few years after Harvey, my mom and I drove by and there was nothing there. No remanants of the T buildings outside or the science lab or the hallways. I was devastated. They had to demolish the whole building to build from scratch. It is hard to lose something you forgot about. All I could remember as we drove past was the rush in my ear and the radio. I can't drive by without crying! Now I can only visit the school in my head. And at somepoint memories become memories of memories. Which isn't fair.
